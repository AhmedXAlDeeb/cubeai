Who We Are

Built to make AI feel practical.

CUBE AI empowers radiologists and imaging teams to develop segmentation models using clinical DICOM studies fetched from PACS (Orthanc). Annotation is assisted by integrated foundation models to speed up label creation.

Training is fully automated via a self-configuring pipeline (nnU-Net + MONAI Label), validated for accuracy, and deployed to a secure model bank for immediate use in assisted segmentation workflows.

Designed for clinical workflows: secure PACS integration, metadata-backed datasets (PostgreSQL), and a web interface that removes the need for AI expertise.

What CUBE AI Does

Six things built right.

PACS Integration

Native DICOM support and integrations with Orthanc and OHIF let you fetch studies directly from PACS for annotation and training.

Assisted Annotation

Interactive annotation tools powered by foundation models (SAM, Vista3D, nnInteractive) accelerate label creation with prompts and corrections.

Self-Configuring Training

Automated training pipelines (nnU-Net + MONAI Label) configure experiments, run validation, and prepare models for deployment.

Model Bank & Deployment

Store trained models centrally and deploy them for inference in assisted segmentation workflows with versioning.

Clinical-Ready

Privacy-first design, PostgreSQL-backed metadata, and audit-friendly workflows make CUBE AI suitable for clinical settings.

Easy Onboarding

Web-based interface lets clinicians and imaging teams start quickly with no AI expertise required.

Research Use Cases

Built for radiology research and reproducible experiments

Use CUBE AI to accelerate retrospective studies, benchmark models, and scale multi-site validation with DICOM-native workflows and reproducible training.

📁

Dataset Curation

Pull studies from Orthanc, filter by metadata, and build clean, provenance-backed datasets for research without manual exports.

✍️

Efficient Labelling

Combine foundation models (SAM) with human correction to generate high-quality labels far faster than manual annotation alone.

🧪

Automated Experiments

Self-configuring nnU-Net training runs experiments with standardized splits and metrics so results are reproducible across runs.

📊

Publish trained models to the model bank, version them, and compare performance across cohorts and sites for robust validation.

🔐

Data Governance

Metadata stored in PostgreSQL and labels pushed back to PACS preserve audit trails and clinical provenance for research audits.
