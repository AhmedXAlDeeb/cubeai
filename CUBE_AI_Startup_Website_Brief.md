# CUBE AI Startup Website Brief

## Purpose

This document defines the content, structure, tone, visual direction, and developer instructions for the CUBE AI public website.

The site should feel like a standard modern startup landing page: clear, focused, credible, and conversion-oriented. Its main goal is to explain what CUBE AI is, show why it matters, and drive visitors to join the early bird waiting list.

## Product Summary

CUBE AI is an AI-powered product built for a real-world use case in this repository. The website should present it as a practical, forward-looking product with a strong identity, not as a generic AI demo.

The website should help visitors answer these questions quickly:

- What is CUBE AI?
- Who is it for?
- What problem does it solve?
- Why should I trust it?
- How do I join the waiting list?

## Brand Theme: CUBE

The entire site should keep the CUBE theme visible in both visuals and content.

### Theme Meaning

- CUBE stands for structure, precision, modularity, and clarity.
- The brand should feel engineered, intelligent, and reliable.
- Visual language should use geometric balance, layered panels, and subtle 3D cube-inspired shapes.

### Visual Direction

- Use a clean, premium startup aesthetic.
- Prefer dark or deep neutral backgrounds with bright accent colors.
- Use cube-like shapes, grids, floating panels, and angled highlights.
- Keep animations smooth and intentional, not flashy.
- Avoid generic AI imagery, overused purple gradients, and stock-photo clutter.

### Suggested Color Direction

- Primary: deep navy or graphite.
- Accent: electric cyan, aqua, or bright blue.
- Secondary accent: soft silver, white, or muted teal.
- Use one strong accent system consistently across the whole site.

### Typography Direction

- Use a modern, readable sans-serif type system.
- Headlines should feel bold and confident.
- Body text should stay simple, short, and easy to scan.

## Website Goals

The site must do the following:

- Introduce CUBE AI in one strong hero section.
- Explain the product value in a few concise blocks.
- Build trust with clear, concrete messaging.
- Convert visitors into early-bird leads.
- Make the startup feel active, modern, and credible.

## Target Audience

The website is for visitors who are:

- Interested in AI products.
- Looking for early access to a new solution.
- Evaluating whether the product fits their workflow or business.
- Potential customers, partners, investors, or early adopters.

## Voice and Tone

The writing should be:

- Clear.
- Confident.
- Brief.
- Human.
- Startup-oriented.

Avoid:

- Overly technical language.
- Buzzword-heavy paragraphs.
- Vague promises like “revolutionary” without explanation.
- Long text blocks that slow down the page.

## Recommended Site Structure

The site should follow this structure.

### 1. Header

Include:

- CUBE AI logo or wordmark.
- Navigation links to the main sections.
- A primary CTA button for the waiting list.

Recommended nav items:

- About
- Features
- How it Works
- Early Bird
- FAQ

### 2. Hero Section

This is the most important section on the page.

Include:

- A strong headline.
- A short supporting paragraph.
- One primary CTA: Join the Early Bird Waiting List.
- One secondary CTA: Learn More or See How It Works.
- A cube-themed visual, abstract product illustration, or layered geometric panel.

Hero messaging direction:

- Describe CUBE AI as a smart, focused AI product.
- Emphasize clarity, speed, and practical value.
- Keep the message short enough to understand in a few seconds.

### 3. Brief About Us

Visitors want a short company introduction.

This section should explain:

- Who we are.
- Why we built CUBE AI.
- What problem we are solving.
- What makes us different.

Keep it short and confident. Do not turn this into a long company story.

### 4. Problem / Value Section

Show the pain point first, then the solution.

Example structure:

- Current challenge.
- Why current options are not enough.
- How CUBE AI improves the experience.

### 5. Core Features

Highlight 3 to 6 key features or benefits.

Each feature should have:

- A short title.
- A one-line explanation.
- A small icon or cube-inspired visual treatment.

Possible feature categories:

- Fast and intelligent workflows.
- Clear results and structured output.
- Easy onboarding.
- Scalable design.
- Secure and reliable experience.
- Built for real user needs.

### 6. How It Works

Add a simple 3-step explanation.

Suggested format:

1. Join the waiting list.
2. Get early access when available.
3. Start using CUBE AI in your workflow.

If the product has a more specific flow, replace this with the real flow.

### 7. Early Bird Waiting List Section

This is the main conversion section.

Required fields:

- Full name.
- Email address.
- Optional company name.
- Optional role or use case.

Required UX behavior:

- The form must be easy to scan and complete.
- Show a clear privacy note.
- Confirm success after submission.
- Handle loading, error, and validation states.

Required CTA copy ideas:

- Join the Early Bird List
- Reserve My Spot
- Get Early Access

### 8. Trust Section

Add light trust signals.

Possible content:

- Security and privacy commitment.
- Early user focus.
- Product built by a serious team.
- Short testimonials if available.
- Partner or roadmap badges if real.

Do not invent fake customers or fake metrics.

### 9. FAQ

Include common questions such as:

- What is CUBE AI?
- Who is it for?
- When will early access open?
- Is the waiting list free?
- How will my data be used?

Answers should be short and direct.

### 10. Footer

Footer should include:

- Logo or wordmark.
- Short brand line.
- Social or contact links.
- Privacy policy link if available.
- Copyright line.

## Content Guidelines

### Headline Style

- Use short, sharp headlines.
- Focus on outcomes and clarity.
- Avoid overdescribing the technology.

### Paragraph Style

- Keep paragraphs short.
- Prefer bullet points over long explanations.
- Put the most important point first.

### CTA Style

- Use one primary CTA per section at most.
- Keep CTA language action-oriented.
- Repeat the waiting list CTA consistently.

## Developer Instructions

### Page Behavior

- Build the site as a responsive landing page.
- Ensure the layout works well on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
- The page should load cleanly and feel polished from first paint.
- Anchor links should scroll smoothly to sections.

### Form Behavior

- Validate the waiting-list form on the client side.
- Prevent duplicate submissions.
- Show clear success and error messages.
- Store or send submissions to the correct backend endpoint when available.
- If backend integration is not ready, isolate the form logic so it can be connected later without redesigning the page.

### UI and Motion

- Use subtle entrance animations for hero and section cards.
- Use staggered reveal effects where helpful.
- Avoid excessive motion on every element.
- Use cube-like hover effects only if they support the theme and do not distract from conversion.

### Layout Rules

- Keep section spacing generous.
- Use strong visual hierarchy.
- Keep the hero above the fold on most screens.
- Make the waiting list CTA visible multiple times.

### Accessibility Rules

- Use semantic HTML.
- Maintain good contrast for text and buttons.
- Support keyboard navigation.
- Add accessible labels for form fields.
- Do not rely on color alone to show state.

### SEO Rules

- Use a descriptive page title.
- Add a relevant meta description.
- Use one H1 only.
- Structure content with proper heading order.
- Add Open Graph metadata for sharing.

### Performance Rules

- Keep images optimized.
- Avoid unnecessary large background assets.
- Prefer SVGs or CSS-based decoration for cube shapes.
- Do not add heavy animation libraries unless needed.

### Analytics and Tracking

- Track waiting list submissions.
- Track CTA clicks.
- Track page scroll depth if analytics is enabled.
- Keep tracking lightweight and privacy-aware.

## Suggested Sections in Order

Use this order unless the team decides otherwise:

1. Header
2. Hero
3. About Us
4. Problem / Value
5. Features
6. How It Works
7. Early Bird Waiting List
8. Trust Section
9. FAQ
10. Footer

## Developer Checklist

- The site uses the CUBE theme consistently.
- The hero section explains the product in seconds.
- The about section is short and credible.
- The feature list is clear and not overloaded.
- The early bird form is easy to find and easy to complete.
- The layout is responsive.
- The design feels premium and startup-ready.
- The page does not look generic or template-like.

## Notes For Future Expansion

If the product grows, the site can later add:

- Product screenshots.
- Customer testimonials.
- Case studies.
- Pricing.
- Blog or updates.
- Investor or partner pages.

For the first release, keep the site simple and conversion-focused.
