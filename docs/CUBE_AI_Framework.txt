1. Executive Summary
CUBE AI is an augmented medical imaging platform developed at Cairo University to automate and orchestrate the full lifecycle of radiology research studies. The platform integrates PACS connectivity, AI-driven segmentation, radiomic feature extraction, machine learning modeling, and automated scientific report generation into a single, natural-language-driven environment.

The system operates in two distinct modes:
• Standard Pipeline Mode: Interactive AI-assisted annotation, region-of-interest refinement, and custom segmentation model training using nnU-Net.
• Agentic Research Mode: A fully autonomous multi-agent system orchestrated via Large Language Model (LLM) reasoning, comprising specialized agents for study planning, cohort management, segmentation, feature extraction, statistical modeling, and automated report generation.

Regulatory Position
CUBE AI is strictly designed for academic research contexts. It is NOT a Software as a Medical Device (SaMD), does not seek FDA 510(k) clearance or CE marking, and must not be used for clinical decision support or real-time patient care.
This document establishes the technical specifications, governance framework, and quality management artifacts required for responsible deployment of CUBE AI in academic research settings. Compliance is addressed against:
• ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management Systems
• FAIR4RS Principles — Research Software Reproducibility
• ALCOA+ Data Integrity Standards — Clinical Data Governance
• Egyptian Personal Data Protection Law No. 151 of 2020 (PDPL) — Legal Compliance

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Compliance Requirements
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the first international standard for AI Management Systems (AIMS). For CUBE AI, it provides the institutional equivalent of ISO 9001 for traditional quality management. Key obligations include:
• AI Policy: Establish a written policy defining ethical principles and research boundaries for AI use within the platform.
• AI Risk Register: Maintain a documented register of identified hazards, including automation bias, data drift, and segmentation errors.
• AI Impact Assessments: Conduct formal assessments before deploying new agent modules or upgrading foundation models.
• Accountable Personnel: Designate governance leads whose responsibilities map to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) role under the PDPL.
• Performance Records: Maintain versioned records of AI system performance; Model Cards serve this function per release.

Clinical Risk-Weighted Error Score (CREST)
The CREST metric provides a clinically meaningful evaluation of model performance by weighting errors according to their clinical significance. Standard accuracy metrics treat all errors equally; CREST weights each error type by its downstream clinical impact:

CREST Formula
CREST = ( ? w? · e? ) / ( ? w? )   where w? = clinical weight of error type i, e? = error rate for that category. Lower CREST scores indicate better clinical alignment.
CREST weights are defined collaboratively between clinical radiologists and the development team for each study type. They are documented in the corresponding Model Card and must be reviewed before each major model release.


3. Technical Specifications
3.1 Foundation Models
CUBE AI leverages three state-of-the-art foundation models for medical image analysis:
• Segment Anything Model (SAM) [5]: Meta's promptable image segmentation foundation model, adapted for interactive medical image annotation. SAM enables click- and box-prompted ROI delineation with real-time feedback.
• Vista3D (MONAI) [6]: NVIDIA's volumetric medical imaging foundation model, providing zero-shot and fine-tuned 3D segmentation across anatomical structures.
• nnU-Net [7]: A self-configuring deep learning framework for biomedical image segmentation that automatically adapts preprocessing pipelines, network architectures, and training procedures to dataset characteristics. Used for custom cohort-specific model training.

3.4 Agentic AI Architecture Design Patterns
CUBE AI's agentic research mode implements the following design patterns from the agentic AI paradigm [9]:
• Goal-Oriented Autonomy: Agents receive high-level research goals expressed in natural language and autonomously decompose them into actionable sub-tasks.
• ReAct Pattern: Agents alternate between reasoning (planning next steps based on partial results) and acting (calling tools) to iteratively solve research problems.
• Hierarchical Orchestration: A Planner agent oversees all specialized agents, coordinates dependencies between tasks, and delegates execution.
• Human-in-the-Loop (HITL): High-risk decisions — including segmentation mask acceptance, cohort inclusion/exclusion, and final report export — require explicit researcher approval.
• Glass-Box Observability: All reasoning steps, tool calls, and intermediate outputs are exposed in a real-time monitoring interface. Researchers can inspect, pause, modify, or redirect any stage.

5.2 Purpose Limitation and Scope Enforcement
Under PDPL Art. 5, personal data must be collected and used only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes. CUBE AI enforces purpose limitation through three complementary mechanisms:
• Study-Specific Data Scopes: Each research study has a defined data access boundary, declared at study inception and enforced by the MCP layer. Agents cannot retrieve data outside the declared scope.
• PICO-Driven Retrieval: All PACS queries are constrained to the Population, Intervention, Comparison, and Outcome parameters formally specified in the study protocol. Ad-hoc queries are not permitted.
• Audit Logging with Purpose Justification: Every data access event is logged with the associated study ID, PICO hash, and the agent that initiated the request.


6. Data Quality Assurance
6.1 Automated Quality Gates
Analogous to incoming quality control (IQC) in pharmaceutical manufacturing, CUBE AI's Data Hygiene Agent enforces data quality before any computational pipeline proceeds. A study cannot advance past a failed gate without explicit researcher override and documented justification:

Quality GateTrigger ConditionSystem ActionMask Coverage CheckSegmentation mask covers < 5% or > 95% of volumePipeline blocked; researcher prompted to review and re-annotate.Inter-Rater ConsistencyCohen's ? < 0.6 between automated and reference masksWarning issued; manual verification required before continuation.DICOM CompletenessMissing required DICOM metadata fieldsRetrieval retried; study flagged as incomplete if unresolvable.Class Imbalance DetectionFewer than 10 positive cases in cohortResearcher warned; study design review strongly recommended.Radiomic StabilityICC < 0.75 for test-retest radiomic featuresUnstable features excluded from downstream modeling; logged in study record.
6.2 Automation Bias Mitigation
CUBE AI enforces an Active Confirmation protocol to prevent automation bias. Researchers must explicitly interact with and verify segmentation masks before they are accepted and propagated downstream. This deliberate friction mechanism ensures that no automated output becomes part of the study record without conscious human review.

Active Confirmation Requirement
No segmentation mask, feature extraction result, or model prediction may be saved to the study record or passed to a downstream agent without explicit researcher sign-off via the CUBE AI interface. This requirement cannot be disabled.

7. Software Verification and Validation
7.1 Verification vs. Validation
• Verification: 'Did we build the system right?' — Unit and integration tests proving each agent calls the correct tools with correct parameters and handles errors gracefully.
• Validation: 'Did we build the right system?' — Clinical research studies confirming CUBE AI outputs align with expert radiologist findings and published clinical standards.

10. Conclusion
This document establishes the technical and governance framework for CUBE AI, providing research-equivalent artifacts for each class of industrial quality certificate. The framework demonstrates that rigorous AI governance is achievable in academic research settings without the overhead of full regulatory medical device clearance.

The platform's glass-box observability architecture, human-in-the-loop design philosophy, and comprehensive MCP-based audit trail together satisfy both regulatory expectations under the Egyptian PDPL and scientific reproducibility requirements under FAIR4RS and ALCOA+. Adherence to ISO/IEC 42001:2023 provides a principled, internationally recognized management system for responsible AI deployment.

This document is a living artifact. It must be reviewed and updated with each model release, system modification, or change in applicable regulations. The next scheduled review is triggered by: (1) any versioned model release, (2) any change to the Egyptian PDPL executive regulations, or (3) annual review, whichever occurs first.
